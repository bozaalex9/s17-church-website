# S17 Website Constitution

## Highest authority

> “The S17 digital experience should never whisper the greatest story ever told. It should proclaim it with order, beauty, urgency, warmth, joy, and loud praise.”

**Psalm 150:3–5**

This statement is the highest design and implementation authority for the S17 website. It replaces the previous S17 Website Constitution entirely. No earlier constitution remains authoritative.

## Constitutional meaning

- **Proclamation:** The gospel and the life of the church deserve confident, memorable expression.
- **Order:** Energy must strengthen hierarchy, comprehension, navigation, and purpose.
- **Beauty:** Craft should honor the message without making spectacle the message.
- **Urgency:** Clear next steps should arrive without hesitation or pressure tactics.
- **Warmth:** People should encounter a family, not a campaign funnel.
- **Joy:** Delight, celebration, color, photography, typography, and motion may carry real energy.
- **Loud praise:** Signature moments may be expansive and exuberant when they serve worship, testimony, welcome, or mission.

## Guardrails

Proclamation must never become manipulation, visual noise, theological imprecision, inaccessible interaction, or performance debt. Sacred stillness remains available where reverence, care, doctrine, grief, prayer, or practical comprehension requires it; it is a purposeful contrast, not the site's default personality.

Every decision should answer:

1. What truth or invitation is being proclaimed?
2. Is its hierarchy unmistakable?
3. Does the expression carry warmth and joy?
4. Can every visitor perceive, understand, navigate, and act?
5. Is the implementation faithful, maintainable, and performant?
