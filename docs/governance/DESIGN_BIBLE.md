# S17 Design Bible

## Governing principle

Design gives visible form to the constitution: confident proclamation shaped by order, beauty, urgency, warmth, joy, and praise.

## Revised principles

- Prefer a clear dominant idea over evenly restrained layouts.
- Permit bold scale, decisive contrast, immersive media, and expressive composition when they clarify the story.
- Use whitespace as a dramatic pause, not as an excuse for timidity.
- Let documentary photography witness to real people, worship, friendship, formation, and mission.
- Use S17's established ink, paper, bronze, burgundy, and white palette with greater confidence; do not import another brand's identity.
- Create continuity between scenes through shared edges, overlapping objects, tone transitions, and visual handoffs.
- Make welcome feel human and joy feel credible rather than polished into advertising.

## Restraint with purpose

Practical information, care, giving, doctrine, forms, and urgent notices prioritize calm comprehension. Their clarity creates contrast for expressive proclamation elsewhere.

## Accessibility

Color contrast, focus visibility, semantic structure, readable measure, predictable navigation, zoom support, and responsive layouts are design requirements—not engineering cleanup.
